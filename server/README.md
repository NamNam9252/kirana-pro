###Kirana

Core Concept: A hyperlocal commerce platform that digitises India's kirana (corner store) ecosystem — turning a simple shop management tool into a full end-to-end commerce, delivery, and operations system.

What it does, layer by layer:
Shop Layer — Shopkeepers register their store, manage inventory, and get a public storefront profile where buyers can browse their items and place orders online.
Commerce Layer — Buyers discover nearby shops, search products (even by intent like "cleaning products"), and order with two fulfilment options: home delivery or takeaway.
Delivery Layer — If the shop has no delivery rider, the platform assigns one. Riders get their own portal to see assignments, navigate to shops and buyers, and manage earnings.
Operations Layer — Shopkeepers can hire workers. Workers get a portal for attendance tracking and seeing their assigned daily tasks.
Supply Layer — A supplier portal handles restocking. When inventory runs low, shops can raise requests to suppliers and track fulfilment — all in one place.
Intelligence Layer — The whole app is AI-native:

An agentic assistant that shopkeepers can talk to ("how much butter is left?")
ML-based demand & supply forecasting
Product recommendation system for buyers
Semantic/sentiment-based product search


One-line pitch: Kirana is the operating system for India's kirana stores — connecting owners, buyers, riders, workers, and suppliers in one agentic platform.
